What Is Football?

Football (also called soccer in some parts of the world) is a sport where two teams of 11 players each compete to score goals by getting a ball into the opposing team's net using any part of their body except their hands and arms (goalkeepers are the exception).

Basic Rules of Football

  • Duration: 90 minutes (two halves of 45 minutes with a break in between).
  • Scoring: A goal is scored when the whole ball crosses the opponent’s goal line.
  • Fouls: Illegal actions (like tripping or handball) result in free kicks, penalties, or cards.
  • Yellow Card: A warning. Two yellow cards equal one red card.
  • Red Card: A player is sent off and cannot be replaced.

Key Positions

  • Goalkeeper: Defends the goal and is the only player allowed to use hands (in the box).
  • Defenders: Prevent the opposing team from scoring.
  • Midfielders: Link defense and attack; control the pace of the game.
  • Forwards/Strikers: Score goals and lead attacks.

Popular Competitions

  • FIFA World Cup
  • UEFA Champions League
  • Premier League (England)
  • La Liga (Spain)
  • CAF Champions League (Africa)

Extra Time & Penalties

If a knockout match ends in a draw, the game may go into extra time (30 minutes), and if still tied, a penalty shootout decides the winner.

Offside Rule (Simplified)

A player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent’s goal than both the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is passed to them — unless they are in their own half or level with the last defender.
